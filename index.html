Biography

Henry Lawson (1867–1922) was one of Australia's most iconic poets and short story writers. Born in Grenfell, New South Wales, he is best known for his depictions of bush life and the struggles of the working class. Partially deaf from a young age, Lawson’s unique voice captured the hardships and spirit of rural Australia during the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

Notable Works

  • "The Drover's Wife"
  • "Joe Wilson and His Mates"
  • "While the Billy Boils"
  • "The Union Buries Its Dead"
  • "The Bush Undertaker"

Legacy

Henry Lawson remains a central figure in Australian literature. His works have influenced generations of writers and are often studied in schools across the country. Monuments, stamps, and literary awards have honored his contributions to Australian culture.
